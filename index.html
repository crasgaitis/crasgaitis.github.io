    PERSONAL PROJECTS

    1. ðŸš§ SmartMusic

      This Synaptech group project aims to generate original music compositions based on neural feedback. First, a user trains their own ML model by listening to music they like and dislike, while wearing an EEG. As they start listening to more music, we can use their neural data and the features of the piece they are listening to, to iteratively improve the music, so that we eventually only output music that the listener enjoys.

    2. âœ… Opticars

      Mini Arduino cars controlled by eye movements - including saccades to set directions and blinks to control stop/go actions.

    3. âœ… Painting with Brainwaves

      I made this Synaptech project on my own to host at UW's "Dawg Daze" events and the "Engineering Launch" event. It is an app that translates your brainwaves into emotions, and then into pictures -- like a waterfall surrounded by birds or a castle on fire! Alternatively, for a less "random" and more rule-based result, I can turn your brainwave activity into fractal patterns with changing colors. I made a nice & intuitive UI for the app too!

    4. âœ… Music and Mental Health (MxMH)

      This was my first solo long-term machine-learning project! The goal was to analyze the correlation (if any) between music taste and mental health. First, I created a survey to gather data (700+ instances) and deployed a website to advertise the project and boost the number of survey responses. Then, I published an exploratory data analysis notebook with my conclusions. I published a second notebook with some ML experiments, aiming to predict depression, anxiety, OCD, and insomnia scores from music habits. Finally, I deployed the top-performing model to the web, which included visualizations comparing its predictions of a new individual to the 700 data points I previously collected.
